⚙ How jscanify detects your document ▼
1
Grayscale + Blur cvtColor + GaussianBlur
Converts RGB to luminance, then applies Gaussian smoothing to remove camera sensor noise before edge detection. Without blurring, tiny pixel variations create hundreds of fake edges.
2
Canny Edge Detection Canny
Computes intensity gradients across the image. Two thresholds (hysteresis): strong edges kept, weak edges kept only if connected to strong ones. Result = thin, 1-pixel-wide edge lines.
3
Morphological Close GAP FIXER
MORPH_CLOSE = dilate then erode. Dilate expands edges and bridges gaps (like where your fingers broke the outline). Erode shrinks back to original thickness but keeps the gap filled. This is why jscanify works better — it includes this step by default.
4
Find Contours findContours
Traces all closed edge loops. Only outer boundaries (RETR_EXTERNAL) — inner document text and lines are ignored entirely.
5
Largest Contour → Polygon Approx approxPolyDP
Picks the contour with the largest area (assuming document is the dominant object). Douglas–Peucker simplification reduces it to 4 points. jscanify also validates the result is a reasonable quadrilateral.
6
Perspective Warp getPerspectiveTransform + warpPerspective
Sorts 4 corners into TL/TR/BR/BL order. Computes a homography matrix mapping them to a flat rectangle. warpPerspective remaps every pixel — output is as if the camera was directly above the document.
7
Why it sometimes still fails
jscanify assumes the document is the largest closed contour. If your body/background is larger = it picks wrong object. Best results: document on a plain contrasting surface, all 4 corners visible, good lighting. If it still fails → use ✋ Manual mode.
